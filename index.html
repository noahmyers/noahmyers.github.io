I'm an accounting professor at Utah Valley University's Woodbury School of Business, where I teach and research at the intersection of accounting and technology. I help accountants navigate the technologies, analytical mindsets, and ethical challenges shaping the future of the profession. In both classrooms and professional settings, I guide students and practitioners through accounting information systems, data analytics, applied AI, and audit methodology.

Before academia, I audited complex IT environments at Ernst & Young and implemented accounting software for mid-market clients at Squire & Company. My research examines how regulation, technology, and information access reshape auditing and assurance.
