I am an Assistant Professor of Accounting at Utah Valley University's Woodbury School of Business. My work sits at the intersection of auditing, technology, and information access—examining how regulation and changing information environments shape assurance and how investors and other stakeholders respond to audit-related events. I teach accounting information systems and accounting data analytics, with an emphasis on systems thinking, internal controls, and using modern tools to analyze and communicate business information.

Speaking & Workshops

I also offer occasional talks and workshops for professional audiences (CPE groups, firms, and student organizations) on auditing, technology, and applied AI in accounting. These sessions are research-informed and practical, typically using concrete cases and demonstrations to help audiences understand how emerging tools and information environments affect accounting work and assurance. Details on formats and sample topics are available on the Speaking & Workshops page.

New Research Sheds Light on the PCAOB's Enforcement Process

Forbes · Sep 8, 2025

The PCAOB's enforcement process is difficult for outsiders to evaluate because it is not sufficiently transparent about how cases are selected and how outcomes align with the Board's investor-protection mandate. A recent Forbes piece highlighted our research and pointed to practical ways to improve transparency—such as clearer evaluation criteria, more explicit discussion of investor harm, and penalty guidance tied to investor impact.

Embracing AI in the Classroom

KSL 5 TV · Aug 28, 2024

I discussed how we are integrating generative AI into accounting coursework at Utah Valley University, including using tools like ChatGPT for spreadsheet automation. I emphasized that AI introduces real risks that require clear guardrails, but that students who learn to use AI as an assistant will be better positioned to focus on higher-judgment work and add value in professional settings.
