About the Speaker

I bring a unique combination of Big 4 audit experience, academic research, and practical technology implementation to every presentation. My goal is to leave audiences with actionable insights they can apply immediately, not just theoretical frameworks.

Before joining academia, I worked in IT Risk Assurance at Ernst & Young and implemented accounting software for mid-market businesses at Squire & Company. I hold a Ph.D. from Arizona State University and am a licensed CPA in Utah.

Whether addressing a room of practicing CPAs, fraud examiners, or graduate students, I design each session to be interactive, relevant, and grounded in real-world examples.

Featured Topic: AI in Accounting & Finance

Artificial intelligence is transforming how accounting and finance professionals work. From automating routine tasks to reshaping audit evidence and financial analysis, AI tools are becoming essential to modern practice.

In this presentation, I explore the practical applications of AI across accounting functions, including:

  • Generative AI for audit and assurance - How tools like large language models are changing the nature of audit evidence and professional judgment
  • Workflow automation - Practical applications of VBA, Python, Power Automate, and Alteryx to eliminate repetitive tasks
  • Risk and opportunity - Understanding what AI can and cannot do, and where human judgment remains essential
  • Preparing for the future - Skills and mindsets professionals need to thrive in an AI-augmented environment

This session can be tailored for CPE credit, executive audiences, or practitioner workshops. I incorporate live demonstrations and hands-on exercises to ensure participants leave with practical, actionable knowledge.

Featured Topic: Lessons from the FTX Collapse

A practical, story-driven breakdown of what went wrong at FTX—and what it teaches accountants, auditors, and finance teams about controls, governance, and professional skepticism in fast-growing organizations.

Using the FTX collapse as a real-world case, I walk through how weak processes, informal systems, and leadership failures can compound into catastrophic risk. I translate the lessons into concrete takeaways: what "good" looks like for basic internal controls, reconciliations, documentation, access/segregation of duties, and auditability—especially when teams are moving fast and relying heavily on spreadsheets and lightweight tools.

This session is designed to be accessible for non-auditors while still delivering actionable insights for assurance and risk professionals.
